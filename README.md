# BMI and Socioeconomic Factors Analysis

This project analyzes the relationship between Body Mass Index (BMI) and socioeconomic factors in the United States. The main objective is to explore which socioeconomic variables appear to have the strongest relationship with BMI outcomes using publicly available survey data.

The analysis focuses on identifying patterns between BMI and variables such as income, education, age, and demographic characteristics.

## Dataset

The analysis uses two datasets that are included in this repository.

Before running the notebook, download both datasets from this repository and make sure they are placed in the same directory as the notebook.

## Conclusions

The analysis suggests that education level is the socioeconomic factor most associated with BMI. Higher levels of educational attainment tend to correspond with lower BMI values.

The second most influential factor appears to be whether the individual was born in the United States. Individuals born in the U.S. tend to show higher BMI values on average compared to those not born in the country.

Although these two variables show the strongest relationships with BMI in the dataset, their impact is not drastically larger than other categories. Most of the remaining socioeconomic variables analyzed do not display a strong or consistent trend with BMI.

Overall, the results suggest that socioeconomic characteristics may influence BMI patterns, but no single factor fully explains BMI variation in the dataset.
